Tortang Talong Recipe

Description

Tortang talong is a Filipino eggplant omelette that delights the palate with its simplicity and rich flavors. Grilled eggplants are flattened, dipped in beaten eggs, and then fried to golden perfection. The resulting dish boast a smoky undertone from the charred eggplant, complemented by the savory blend of eggs. Often seasoned with salt and pepper, and occasionally enhanced with minced garlic or onions, tortang talong is a satisfying and versatile dish, served as a side, main course, or even as a filling for sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • Eggplant:1 piece chinese eggplant
  • Egg:1 piece
  • Salt:1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Black Pepper:1/8 teaspoon grounded
  • Oil:1/4 cup cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Eggplant:
      Heat the eggplant directly over fire in a stovetop until the skin turns brown to black, rotate after a few minutes until all parts of are almost burnt. Then let the eggplant cool down and then peel of the skin using your fingers. The inside part of the eggplant should be steamed just right at this point. Arrange the peeled eggplant in a plate and then flatten using a fork. Set aside.
  2. Egg:
      Beat the egg in a bowl and add salt and pepper. Continue beating until the salt and pepper are well blended. Dip the eggplant in the mixture.
  3. Oil:
      Heat oil in a pan. Once the oil gets hot, start to fry one side of the eggplant until it gets medium brown. Pour the remaining egg mixture on top of the eggplant. Turn it over and fry the other side until it gets light to medium brown. And don't forget to serve with banana ketchup.
